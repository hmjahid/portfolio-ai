Posting the right topics is critical if you want your content to bring WordPress development clients instead of just likes. The best posts focus on business problems that your services solve. 🚀

Here are the types of topics that actually attract clients.

1️⃣ Case Study Posts (Best for Clients)

These posts show real results, which builds trust.

Example topics:

“How I reduced a WordPress website load time from 7s → 1.5s”

“How I migrated a WordPress site to cloud hosting with zero downtime”

“How I fixed a broken WordPress website in 30 minutes”

“How I optimized a slow website using Redis caching”

Why it works
Clients see proof of expertise.

2️⃣ Common Website Mistakes

Businesses love these posts because they realize their website has problems.

Example topics:

“5 mistakes that make WordPress websites slow”

“Why most business websites fail to generate leads”

“7 security mistakes in WordPress websites”

“Why cheap hosting destroys your website performance”

Tools you can mention:

WordPress

Cloudflare

3️⃣ Educational Posts

These help establish you as an expert.

Example topics:

“What makes a high-converting business website”

“What is CDN and why every website should use one”

“How caching improves WordPress performance”

“What is Core Web Vitals and why it matters”

Example tools:

Cloudflare

LiteSpeed Cache

4️⃣ Website Audit Posts

These attract clients very effectively.

Example topics:

“I audited a random business website — here are 5 problems”

“Website teardown: fixing performance and UX issues”

“Live WordPress speed audit”

Tools to show results:

GTmetrix

PageSpeed Insights

5️⃣ Before / After Transformation

Visual results attract attention.

Example topics:

“Before vs after: WordPress speed optimization”

“Redesigning a business website for better conversions”

“Website performance transformation”

These posts show value clearly.

6️⃣ Pricing / Business Advice

These attract business owners.

Example topics:

“How much does a professional WordPress website cost in 2026?”

“Why cheap websites cost businesses more money”

“How a good website increases leads for businesses”

These posts bring serious clients.

7️⃣ Quick Tips (Short Viral Posts)

These build visibility.

Example topics:

“3 plugins I install on every WordPress website”

“3 ways to make WordPress faster instantly”

“3 things every business website must have”

Example tools:

WordPress

Elementor

8️⃣ DevOps + WordPress Posts (Your Unique Advantage)

Very few developers post about this combination.

Example topics:

“Running WordPress with Docker”

“Monitoring WordPress servers using Prometheus”

“Scaling WordPress with Kubernetes”

This positions you as a high-level expert.

9️⃣ Client Problem Stories

Stories connect emotionally.

Example topics:

“A client came to me with a hacked WordPress website”

“A business was losing leads because their website was slow”

“How a website redesign doubled a company’s inquiries”

Stories are very engaging.

🔟 Lead Magnet Posts

These encourage people to contact you.

Example topics:

“Comment ‘AUDIT’ and I will review your website”

“I’m offering 5 free website audits this week”

“DM me if your website is slow”

These posts generate direct leads.

The Secret to Client-Attracting Posts

Every post should answer this question:

“How does this help a business owner?”

If a post helps them:

get more leads
